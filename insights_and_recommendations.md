# 📊 Insights & Recommendations
## Customer Shopping Behavior Analysis

---

## 📋 Page 1 — Overview

### 🔍 Key Insights

**1. Young Adults are the biggest revenue driver**
Young Adult is the highest-revenue age group, contributing the most to total revenue of $233.1K. This suggests that the majority of purchasing power comes from younger customers.

**2. Clothing dominates all categories**
Clothing leads both in revenue ($104K) and sales count (1,700+ transactions), significantly outperforming Accessories ($74K), Footwear ($36K), and Outerwear ($19K).

**3. Low subscription adoption**
Only 27% of 3,900 customers have an active subscription, meaning 73% are non-subscribers. This represents a major untapped opportunity for the business.

**4. Healthy average spend**
The average purchase amount is $59.76, indicating customers are willing to spend a moderate-to-high amount per transaction.

### 💡 Recommendations

- **Target Young Adults** with personalized marketing campaigns and loyalty rewards to maintain and grow this key segment.
- **Invest more in Clothing** — expand product variety and stock levels as it is the clear top-performing category.
- **Launch a subscription drive** — with only 27% adoption, offering exclusive discounts or perks for subscribers could significantly increase recurring revenue.
- **Cross-sell Outerwear and Footwear** to existing Clothing buyers to boost revenue in underperforming categories.

## 📦 Page 2 — Product Analysis

### 🔍 Key Insights

**1. Blouse is the star product**
Blouse ranks #1 in both sales count and total revenue, making it the most valuable product in the entire catalog. It is followed by Shirt and Jacket.

**2. Jewelry tops the rating chart**
Despite not being the best seller, Jewelry has the highest average review rating (3.86), indicating strong customer satisfaction. Gloves and Sandals also perform well in ratings.

**3. Outerwear has the highest discount rate**
Outerwear leads in discount rate at 44.44%, followed by Accessories (43.79%), Footwear (43.24%), and Clothing (42.08%). Overall, 41.91% of all orders received a discount.

**4. Fall is the strongest season**
Fall generates the highest revenue (~$59K), while Summer is the weakest season (~$56K), suggesting seasonal demand fluctuations.

**5. Size M dominates purchases**
45% of all purchases are Size M, followed by L (27%), S (17%), and XL (11%).

### 💡 Recommendations

- **Promote Blouse year-round** as the flagship product — bundle it with lower-performing items to increase average order value.
- **Leverage Jewelry's high rating** — use customer reviews in marketing materials to attract new buyers.
- **Review Outerwear discount strategy** — with a 44.44% discount rate, ensure profitability is not being sacrificed. Consider reducing discounts and focusing on value-based promotions instead.
- **Prepare for Fall demand** — increase inventory and launch seasonal campaigns before Fall to maximize revenue during peak season.
- **Optimize for Summer** — introduce Summer-specific promotions or products to close the revenue gap with other seasons.
- **Prioritize Size M and L stock** — together they account for 72% of all purchases.

## 👥 Page 3 — Customer Analysis

### 🔍 Key Insights

**1. Overwhelming majority are Loyal customers**
79.05% of customers are classified as Loyal (more than 15 previous purchases), with only 14.2% Returning and 6.75% New. This shows a highly retained customer base.

**2. Non-subscribers spend more than subscribers**
Surprisingly, non-subscribed customers have a higher total spend (~$40K) compared to subscribed customers (~$20K). This may indicate that the subscription program is not attracting high-value customers.

**3. Senior customers purchase most frequently**
Senior age group has the highest purchase frequency, buying approximately every 90 days on average. Young Adults purchase least frequently.

**4. Adult age group spends the most per transaction**
Despite Young Adults driving the most revenue overall, Adults have the highest average spend per transaction (~$62), suggesting they buy fewer but higher-value items.

**5. Payment methods are evenly distributed**
PayPal is the most popular payment method (17.54%), but all 6 payment methods are used fairly equally (15–18%), indicating no strong payment preference among customers.

**6. Gender purchase frequency is similar**
Male and Female customers have nearly identical average purchase frequency (~85–90 days), showing no significant behavioral difference between genders.

### 💡 Recommendations

- **Focus retention efforts on Loyal customers** — implement a VIP rewards program to keep the 79% Loyal segment engaged and prevent churn.
- **Re-evaluate the subscription program** — since non-subscribers spend more, consider restructuring subscription benefits to attract higher-value customers (e.g., exclusive early access, free shipping).
- **Target Senior customers with frequency-based campaigns** — they already buy regularly, so loyalty perks could increase their average spend per transaction.
- **Upsell Adult customers** — since Adults spend the most per transaction, premium product recommendations and bundles could further increase their order value.
- **Simplify payment options** — since all payment methods are used equally, ensure all 6 methods are seamless and well-supported to avoid friction at checkout.
- **Launch a New Customer acquisition campaign** — with only 6.75% New customers, the business needs to attract fresh buyers to sustain long-term growth.
